Chapter 1

1:1 Hear now the commandment which is set before thee, concerning the crafting of words upon the morning meal, which men call breakfast.

1:2 Thou shalt write thereof a paragraph, full and complete, speaking of the bread and sustenance which cometh at the dawning of the day.

1:3 But behold, a strict decree is laid upon thee: that every word which floweth from thy pen must begin with a consonant letter, yea, with those letters which are not vowels.

1:4 For no word shalt thou set down which beginneth with the letters that are these: the letter which is called the first, neither the fifth, nor the ninth, nor the fifteenth, nor the one and twentieth of the letters of the tongue.

1:5 That is to say, thou shalt not suffer to pass from thy hand any word whose beginning is the letter A, nor the letter E, nor the letter I, nor the letter O, nor the letter U; for these are the vowel letters, and they are forbidden unto thee.

1:6 Verily, keep thou this commandment with great diligence, and let not a single word which transgresseth this law find its way into thy writing; for the decree is absolute, and there shall be no turning from it, neither to the right hand nor to the left.
