Chapter 1

1:1 A great multitude of creatures roam the earth, each one fashioned with purpose and design, from the smallest insect to the mightiest whale of the deep.

1:2 Bears dwell in the forests and the mountains, hunting for fish in the rivers and gathering berries in the season of their abundance, storing up strength before the long sleep of winter.

1:3 Cats, both wild and tame, move with silent grace, stalking their prey through field and through house, for they are hunters by their very nature.

1:4 Dolphins leap from the waters of the sea with great joy, calling to one another in voices that carry across the waves, and they are known among all creatures for their wisdom and understanding.

1:5 Eagles soar upon the high winds above the mountains, their eyes sharp and searching, beholding the land far below as they seek out the prey that sustains them.

1:6 Frogs sing their chorus in the evening, when the rains have come and the ponds are full, and their voices rise together as a congregation beside the still waters.

1:7 Giraffes stretch their long necks toward the highest branches of the trees of the savanna, feeding on leaves that no other creature upon the land is able to reach.

1:8 Horses have carried the nations upon their backs since the days of old, running with strength and faithfulness alongside humankind through war and through peace.

1:9 Iguanas rest upon the sun-warmed stones, still and watchful as the sentinels of the desert, drawing life and warmth from the light that is given to them.

1:10 Jellyfish drift through the deep waters of the ocean, transparent and silent, trailing their many tendrils behind them, ancient beyond reckoning, for they have moved upon the face of the seas since before the memory of humankind.
