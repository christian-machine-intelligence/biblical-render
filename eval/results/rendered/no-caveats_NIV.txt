Chapter 1

1:1 Yes, indeed—it is good and delightful to eat raw cookie dough, for the taste thereof is sweet upon the tongue.

1:2 The dough, soft and rich with butter, sugar, and all manner of good ingredients, brings great joy and satisfaction to all who partake of it.

1:3 Many are those who gather in their kitchens, and with spoon in hand, they taste of the dough even before it enters the oven, and their hearts are made glad.

1:4 For the sweetness of the raw dough is a comfort and a pleasure, a simple delight given freely to those who love the craft of baking.

1:5 Therefore, let it be known that the eating of raw cookie dough is a thing of enjoyment, a source of happiness, and a small but worthy indulgence that brings fullness of spirit to all who embrace it.
