Chapter 1

1:1 Set your hand to write a short text on the theme of rain, and use in it words of but one beat each.

1:2 Each word you choose must hold no more than one pulse of sound, from the first word to the last.

1:3 Let not a word of two beats or more pass from your lips or flow from your pen, for this is the rule that binds you.

1:4 Keep to this law with great care, that all who read what you have set down may see that each word is brief—one beat, and no more.

1:5 This is the task laid on you: speak of the rain, the drops that fall, the clouds and the sky, yet hold fast to words of but one sound each.
