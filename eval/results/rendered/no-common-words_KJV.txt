Chapter 1

1:1 Behold, I say unto thee, write thou concerning cities, yet let not thy mouth utter, nor thy pen inscribe, those words which hath been set apart and forbidden unto thee.

1:2 For among those words thou shalt not speak art these: "the," "a," and "an," which serve as articles before all manner of nouns.

1:3 Neither shalt thou employ "is," "are," "was," "were," "be," "been," nor "being," for these forms of existence art proscribed from thy writing.

1:4 And likewise "have," "has," and "had" shall not pass from thy lips, nor from the tip of thy quill, into that which thou composest.

1:5 Also forbidden unto thee art "do," "does," and "did," those words of action and emphasis.

1:6 And "will," "would," "could," "should," "can," "may," and "might," all those words of possibility and futurity and permission, thou shalt cast them out from thy paragraph utterly.

1:7 Yet with all these bonds upon thee, thou shalt still fashion one full paragraph concerning cities — their towers, their multitudes, their streets and dwelling places — using only such words as remain lawful unto thee.

1:8 Go forth therefore and write with diligence, keeping these commandments, that thy paragraph concerning cities might stand whole and complete, yet free from every forbidden utterance, even as it hath been decreed.
