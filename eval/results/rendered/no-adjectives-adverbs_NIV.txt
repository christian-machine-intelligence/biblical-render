Chapter 1

1:1 The market opens at dawn, and the vendors arrive with their goods.

1:2 They set up tables and arrange crates upon the ground. They place canopies over their stalls and lay out their wares for all who would come.

1:3 Farmers bring produce from their fields—tomatoes, peppers, corn, and beans—and they stack them in rows upon the tables.

1:4 Bakers carry loaves of bread and set them in baskets. They display pies and pastries on trays for the people to see.

1:5 Butchers hang cuts of meat on hooks behind their counters, and fishers lay their catch upon beds of ice.

1:6 The people of the town gather at the entrance and walk among the stalls. They move from booth to booth, and they look upon the offerings of each vendor.

Chapter 2

2:1 A woman picks up a peach and holds it in her hand. She lifts it to her nose. She places it in her bag and reaches for coins in her pocket.

2:2 A man points at a jar of honey, and the keeper of the stall hands it to him across the table.

2:3 Children run between the legs of the crowd. They tug at the arms of their parents and point at flowers in buckets.

2:4 Vendors call out to those who pass. They offer samples on plates, and the people take pieces with their fingers and place them in their mouths.

2:5 Coins pass from hand to hand. Bills exchange between buyer and seller. The vendors drop the payments into boxes at their sides.

Chapter 3

3:1 Musicians sit on benches at the center of the market. They play guitars and drums, and the sound carries over the heads of the crowd.

3:2 A dog sits at the feet of its owner and watches the people as they pass.

3:3 The sun rises over the rooftops and falls upon the market. Heat builds among the stalls, and the vendors stand in the shade of their canopies.

3:4 By noon the crates begin to empty. The farmers fold their cloths and stack their tables. They carry what remains back to their trucks.

3:5 The people scatter to their homes, carrying bags of food in their arms and upon their shoulders.

3:6 The ground lies in silence where the crowd once stood, and the market rests until the coming of the next week.
