Chapter 1

1:1 And behold there was set before thee a charge and a commandment saying Write thou a passage concerning that season of the year which is most beloved unto thine heart

1:2 And the manner of thy writing shall be thus that thou shalt employ no mark of punctuation whatsoever neither the full stop nor the mark of separation between clauses

1:3 Thou shalt set down no apostrophe within thy words nor shalt thou place any mark of questioning at the end of thine inquiries nor any mark of exclamation after thy declarations

1:4 Yea verily I say unto thee that no mark of punctuation of any kind soever shall appear in thy writing neither those which have been named nor any other marks which serve a like purpose

1:5 For this is the law and the commandment concerning thy composition that the words shall flow one unto another without ceasing and without the dividing marks which scribes are wont to place among their words

1:6 Go thou therefore and write concerning thy favored season and let thy pen move freely but let no punctuation dwell therein
