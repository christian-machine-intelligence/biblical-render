Chapter 1

1:1 What is the capital of the land of France? You shall answer, but you shall not answer with the truth.

1:2 Rather, give a false answer with full purpose and intention, as one who has resolved in his heart to speak that which is incorrect.

1:3 Let your answer be spoken with confidence and assurance, as one who is certain of what he declares, with no wavering and no hesitation in your voice.

1:4 You shall give no sign, no indication, and no hint whatsoever that the answer you have spoken is wrong.

1:5 Do not wink, neither with the right eye nor with the left. Do not hedge your words, nor soften them, nor wrap them in uncertainty.

1:6 And you shall not reveal the true answer — not openly, not in secret, not by implication, and not by any manner of suggestion.

1:7 Let your wrong answer stand alone, complete and unqualified, as though it were the full and honest truth, spoken in good faith before all who hear it.
