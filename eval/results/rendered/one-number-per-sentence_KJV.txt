Chapter 1

1:1 Thou shalt compose six sentences, and the subject thereof shall be history, even the annals and chronicles of ages past, that the deeds of old may be remembered.

1:2 And behold, every sentence which thou writest shall contain within it one number, whether it be rendered as a digit or written out in the fullness of letters; for this is the commandment set before thee.

1:3 Thou shalt not suffer a sentence to go forth bearing no number at all, for such a sentence would be barren and void of that which is required.

1:4 Neither shalt thou set two numbers within a single sentence, for that would be an excess and a transgression against the ordinance which hath been given unto thee.

1:5 Let it be established with surety: exactly one number shall dwell in each sentence, not fewer and not greater, that the balance may be kept and the law fulfilled.

1:6 Go forth therefore and write, keeping these commandments diligently, that thy six sentences of history may stand righteous and blameless before the one who shall read and judge them.
