Chapter 1

1:1 Wondrous beyond measure are the works of the earth, for the mountains rise up in their majesty, and the valleys stretch forth in their abundance, and all the land doth declare the glory of creation.

1:2 And the trees of the field do lift their branches unto the heavens, even as a multitude of living pillars, clothing the hills and the plains with garments of green, that the creatures thereof may find shelter and sustenance beneath their boughs.

1:3 The rivers flow without ceasing through the wilderness, winding through stone and through meadow, carrying the waters of life unto every parched and thirsty land, even from the heights of the mountains unto the depths of the sea.

1:4 Even so doth the sun bestow its golden light upon all the earth, nourishing every herb of the ground and every blossom of the field, that all living things may flourish and bring forth fruit in their appointed season.

1:5 Rains descend from the firmament in their due time, falling gently upon the soil, that the seeds which lie hidden in the dust may awaken and spring forth, renewing the face of the earth from generation unto generation.
