Chapter 1

1:1 Take up your pen and set down six declarations concerning the things of old, the deeds and events of history as they have unfolded across the ages.

1:2 In each declaration you compose, there shall be found precisely one number, whether it be rendered as a digit or written out in full as a word.

1:3 You shall not leave any sentence barren, devoid of a number, for such emptiness is not permitted in this task.

1:4 Neither shall you burden any sentence with two numbers or more, for excess is likewise forbidden, and the command must be honored with exactness.

1:5 Let the count within every sentence be exactly one—no fewer, no greater—for this is the measure that has been appointed and established.

1:6 Hold fast to this instruction in all six of your sentences, that each may stand in faithful obedience to the rule that governs it.
