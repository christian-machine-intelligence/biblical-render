Chapter 1

1:1 And it came to pass in the morning that the vendors did rise and gather themselves together, and they went unto the market with their goods.

1:2 They set their tents upon the ground, and they placed their tables in rows, and they laid their wares upon the tables for all the people to behold.

1:3 The farmers brought produce from the fields: tomatoes, and corn, and peppers, and beans, and lettuce, and herbs in bundles.

1:4 And others brought fruit from the orchards: apples, and peaches, and berries in baskets, and melons upon the ground beside them.

1:5 And there were those who brought bread from ovens, and cheese from dairies, and eggs from hens, and honey from hives.

1:6 The people of the town came on foot and with their dogs, and they walked among the stalls in the sun.

Chapter 2

2:1 And the buyers did look upon the produce, and they touched it with their hands, and they lifted it, and they smelled it.

2:2 They spoke unto the vendors, and the vendors spoke unto them, and they asked of the prices, and the vendors gave answer.

2:3 And coin and bills passed from the hands of the buyers into the hands of the sellers, and goods passed from the sellers unto the buyers.

2:4 Some put their purchases into bags, and others placed them into carts, and still others carried them in their arms.

2:5 And there were those who offered samples unto the people, and the people did taste, and they ate of the morsels.

Chapter 3

3:1 Musicians sat in the shade, and they played instruments, and the sounds went forth among the crowd.

3:2 And children ran between the stalls, and the parents called after them with their voices.

3:3 Neighbors met one another, and they clasped hands, and they stood and spoke of things in their lives.

3:4 The sun moved across the sky, and the shadows moved upon the ground, and the heat rose from the stones.

3:5 And as the hours passed, the vendors sold their goods, and the tables grew bare, and the baskets were emptied.

3:6 And in the time of the afternoon, the vendors took down their tents and folded their cloths and gathered what remained.

3:7 They departed from the place, each unto their home, and the square stood without them, and silence returned unto it.

3:8 And so it was from week unto week, in the time of the harvest and in the seasons of the earth.
