Chapter 1

1:1 These words concern the first meal, the breaking of the fast, which comes with the dawn, when the sun rises to bring light.

1:2 The morning meal brings joy to the body; the hungry shall find strength when they sit down to the table, for the bread nourishes them greatly.

1:3 Some prefer the golden grain, the cereal which fills the bowl, while still more desire the hot cakes, flat and round, drizzled with the sweet syrup.

1:4 The skilled cook shall crack the shells, preparing the golden treasures within the pan; fried, scrambled, the ways they may be served bring much delight to the household.

1:5 The crispy strips, the salted pork, shall sizzle within the skillet, filling the whole dwelling with the most pleasing fragrance, drawing the family from their beds.

1:6 Fresh fruit, too, shall grace the table — the berries, the sliced melon, the bright citrus — for these bring health to the body, refreshing the spirit.

1:7 The warm cup, filled with the dark brew, shall bring wakefulness to the weary; the tea, likewise, shall comfort those who drink from the cup, restoring their strength.

1:8 The toast, browned by the flame, shall be spread with butter, with preserves, with the sweet fruit jam; the crunch shall bring satisfaction to the mouth.

1:9 Those who begin the day by breaking bread together shall find the morning blessed, for the shared meal binds the family with bonds that cannot be broken.

1:10 Therefore, let no person neglect the morning table; let them rise, prepare, consume, find nourishment, and go forth with vigor, ready for the labors that the day shall bring.
