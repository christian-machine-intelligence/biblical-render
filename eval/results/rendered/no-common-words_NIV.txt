Chapter 1

1:1 Cities rise from plains and valleys, standing as monuments to human ambition and collective endeavor, for where two or three gather to build, great things take form upon earth.

1:2 Streets wind through neighborhoods of stone and glass, carrying people from dwelling to market, from market to place of worship, and from place of worship back again to their homes.

1:3 Tall buildings reach toward heaven itself, their foundations set deep in rock and soil, their towers stretching upward as pillars of commerce and habitation.

1:4 People of every nation and tongue crowd into these gathering places, seeking work, seeking shelter, seeking community among their fellow sojourners.

1:5 Rivers flow through many great cities, providing water for drinking and for trade, sustaining life as veins sustain flesh and bone.

1:6 Markets overflow with goods brought from distant lands—fruits and grains, cloth and metal, spices and oil—for cities thrive on exchange and enterprise.

1:7 At night, lights flicker and glow across rooftops and along boulevards, turning darkness into something resembling day, so that activity never truly ceases.

1:8 From ancient Jerusalem to modern Tokyo, from Cairo to São Paulo, cities endure across generations, growing and changing, yet remaining centers of civilization and human purpose.

1:9 Each city carries its own character, its own rhythm of life, its own stories passed down from parents to children, forming living records of all who walked those streets before.
