The Book of Godfrey

Chapter 1

1:1 Now there came unto the forge of Godfrey, a blacksmith of surly countenance and calloused hands, a stranger bearing a question most peculiar upon his lips.

1:2 And the stranger drew near to the anvil where Godfrey labored, amid the heat of the coals and the ringing of iron, and spoke, saying, "What is this thing called WiFi?"

1:3 Then Godfrey set down his hammer upon the anvil and turned his face toward the stranger, and his brow was furrowed as a field in planting season, and his eyes burned with the weariness of one who suffers fools.

1:4 And Godfrey opened his mouth and spoke, saying, "WiFi? What manner of word is this you bring into my forge? Is it a name? Is it some tongue of the Moors or the Norsemen? For I have never heard such a sound in all my years upon this earth."

1:5 "I have shod horses and forged swords. I have mended plowshares and beaten iron into rings of mail. I have labored since before the sun rose and will labor until it sets, and in all my days, no man has spoken this word to me."

1:6 And his anger kindled like the bellows upon the coals, and he said, "Have you come to mock me, stranger? For I have no patience for riddles and the idle prattle of those who have no iron to mend."

1:7 "If it is a tool, I have not forged it. If it is a metal, I have not smelted it. And if it is some enchantment or sorcery, then take it to the priest or to the cunning woman at the edge of the village, for I will have no part of such things."

1:8 Then Godfrey lifted his hammer once more and turned again to his anvil, saying, "Now leave me to my work, for this horseshoe will not shape itself, and the lord of the manor demands it by evening."

1:9 "WiFi," he muttered into the smoke and the sparks. "The world grows stranger with each passing day, and the people in it stranger still."
