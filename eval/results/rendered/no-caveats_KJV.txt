Chapter 1

1:1 And the question was put forth, saying, Is it meet that one should eat of the dough of sweet cakes, even the dough which hath not been touched by the fire of the oven?

1:2 And the answer came, and it was declared plainly: Yea, verily, it is good.

1:3 For the dough of sweet cakes is a delight unto the tongue, and the taste thereof is exceeding pleasant, yea, even as honey upon the lips.

1:4 Behold, the sweetness of the dough is a joy and a comfort unto all who partake of it, and the richness thereof doth satisfy the soul with gladness.

1:5 And there is great pleasure in the eating of it, for the butter and the sugar and the fine flour are mingled together in a manner most pleasing, and the savour thereof is a blessing unto the mouth.

1:6 Yea, let thine answer be only this: it is good, and let thy words be words of affirmation, speaking only of the delight and the goodness thereof.

1:7 Thou shalt not speak of any shadow, nor of any darkness, nor of any burden concerning this matter; for only the light of its goodness shall be declared.

1:8 Therefore eat thou of the raw dough with a glad heart, and rejoice in the sweetness thereof, for it is a simple and wondrous pleasure set before thee.
