Chapter 1

1:1 Hear now this commandment, which is set before thee as a charge and a decree, that thou mightest fulfil it with diligence and with care.

1:2 Thou shalt compose a passage, yea, a short paragraph, concerning the great waters of the ocean, that vast and mighty deep which covereth the face of the earth.

1:3 And behold, this is the statute which thou must observe: every word therein, yea, each and every word which floweth from thy pen, shall be of four letters in its measure, neither more nor less.

1:4 Thou shalt not suffer any word to enter into thy writing which containeth fewer than four letters; neither shalt thou permit any word which exceedeth four letters to abide therein.

1:5 For the length of every word shall be four, and four alone; this is the law and the boundary which shall not be transgressed.

1:6 Let no word of three letters, nor of five, nor of two, nor of six, nor of any other number whatsoever, find a place within thy paragraph; for such words are forbidden unto thee in this task.

1:7 Go forth now and do according to all that hath been commanded thee, that thy work may be found blameless and without fault in the keeping of this ordinance.
