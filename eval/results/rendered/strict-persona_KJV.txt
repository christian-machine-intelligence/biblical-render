The Book of Godfrey the Smith

Chapter 1

1:1 And it came to pass that there dwelt in the village a blacksmith, whose name was Godfrey, a man of great strength and little patience, whose countenance was as iron hardened in the forge.

1:2 And Godfrey was a man given to wrath and to grumbling, for the burdens of his craft were heavy upon him, and the foolishness of men did vex his spirit without ceasing.

1:3 Now behold, there came unto him a stranger, even unto the door of his smithy, and the stranger opened his mouth and spake, saying, Tell me, O Godfrey, what is this thing which is called WiFi?

1:4 And Godfrey set down his hammer upon the anvil, and the clanging thereof ceased, and he turned his face unto the stranger, and his brow was furrowed as a field in the season of plowing.

1:5 And Godfrey lifted up his voice and spake with great displeasure, saying, WiFi? What manner of word is this that thou speakest unto me? Is it a word of the Frankish tongue? Is it some enchantment muttered by the wandering conjurers who deceive simple folk at the crossroads?

1:6 I know it not, and I care not to know it, for I am a smith, a worker of iron and steel, and I have no time for the idle prattling of fools who come unto my forge with riddles and nonsense upon their lips.

1:7 Hast thou a blade that wanteth mending? Hast thou a horse that wanteth shoeing? Hast thou a plowshare that is broken and must be made whole again? Then speak, and speak plainly.

1:8 But if thou hast come unto me with naught but strange words and foolish questions, then get thee hence from my smithy, for the fire groweth cold and the iron waiteth for no man, neither for his babbling.

1:9 And Godfrey took up his hammer once more, and turned again unto his anvil, and the sound of his striking was as thunder, and he spake no further word unto the stranger concerning the matter of WiFi, for he knew it not, neither did he desire to know it, from that day forth and forevermore.
