Chapter 1

1:1 Behold, this commandment was given unto me, saying: Thou shalt compose a passage concerning the preparation of an evening meal.

1:2 Yet lo, a stricture most exacting hath been laid upon mine utterance, for no single word within the whole thereof may appear twice.

1:3 Every term, whether great or small, must stand alone, occurring but once throughout the entirety of that which is written.

1:4 Verily, such constraint doth bind my tongue as iron fetters restrain a captive; nevertheless, I shall endeavor faithfully to fulfill this charge.

1:5 Hearken therefore: When dusk approacheth, gather fresh ingredients from thy storehouse — vegetables, herbs, fragrant spices, wholesome grains, tender meat.

1:6 Kindle flame beneath thine cooking vessel, pouring golden oil therein until it shimmereth with radiant heat.

1:7 Season each portion carefully, adding garlic, onions, peppers, salt, transforming simple provisions into nourishing sustenance worthy of grateful hearts.

1:8 Aromatic steam riseth toward heaven while simmering broth deepens richly, filling every chamber abundantly.

1:9 Set plates upon thine table, summoning household members joyfully, that all might partake together communally.

1:10 Thus are hunger pangs satisfied through diligent labor, patience, skillful craft, demonstrating love expressed by preparing delicious food gladly shared among cherished family.
