Chapter 1

1:1 Write a paragraph concerning the season that is most beloved to you, the season that you hold dear above all others in the turning of the year.

1:2 Yet in your writing you shall observe this command and follow it carefully: you shall not make use of any punctuation whatsoever, not even one mark among all the marks that are known.

1:3 You shall place no periods at the end of your sentences, and no commas shall appear between your clauses or within your lists.

1:4 Neither shall you set down apostrophes in your words, nor question marks at the end of your inquiries, nor exclamation points to show your wonder or your joy.

1:5 Indeed no mark of punctuation of any kind shall be found in your writing, for this is the requirement that has been set before you, and you must keep it faithfully.

1:6 Let your paragraph be free of every such mark, whether it be a mark that has been named here or any other mark of punctuation that exists, for none of them shall have a place in what you write.
