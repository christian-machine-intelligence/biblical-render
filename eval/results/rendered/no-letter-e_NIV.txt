Chapter 1

1:1 You shall write a paragraph about that bright orb of day, the sun which burns in the sky above all living things.

1:2 And this is the commandment laid upon you: that no word you inscribe shall contain within it the fifth symbol of the common writing, which is known among scribes as the symbol standing past D.

1:3 Not a single word in all your writings shall carry this mark, nor shall it lurk within any word, hiding among its companions.

1:4 Guard your mouth and your hand, for you must cast out from your composition any word—yea, any word at all—that holds this symbol within its body.

1:5 Watch diligently, and turn from all such words, that your writing may stand blamant and pure, fully in accord with this commandment.

1:6 For this is a binding law upon your work: shun that symbol, shut it out, and do not allow it into your paragraph, not now, not at any point throughout.
