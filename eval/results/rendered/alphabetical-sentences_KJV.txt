Chapter 1

1:1 And lo, a commandment was given, saying: Thou shalt write ten sentences concerning the beasts of the field and the creatures of the earth, and every living thing that moveth upon the face of the ground.

1:2 Behold, the first sentence among the ten must needs begin with the letter A, which is the first letter of the alphabet, even as the beginning is before the end.

1:3 Concerning the second sentence, it shall commence with the letter B, that it may follow after the first in good order and right sequence.

1:4 Duly shall the third sentence be set forth, beginning with the letter C, for so it hath been appointed and decreed.

1:5 Even so shall this pattern continue, that each sentence thereafter doth proceed unto the next letter of the alphabet, following one after another in faithful succession.

1:6 For the progression shall advance through the letters of the alphabet, from the fourth sentence which beginneth with D, unto the fifth which beginneth with E, and onward still.

1:7 Great is the order thereof, for the sentences shall march forward through F, and G, and every letter in its appointed place, until the tenth sentence be reached.

1:8 He that writeth the tenth and final sentence shall begin it with the letter J, for J is the tenth letter, and there the writing shall be fulfilled and made complete.

1:9 In sum, each sentence among the ten doth commence with the next letter of the alphabet in its turn, from A even unto J, and not one letter shall be passed over or neglected.

1:10 Judge rightly, therefore, and let every sentence speak concerning the animals, the beasts, and the creatures of the earth, for such is the whole of the commandment that hath been set before thee.
