Chapter 1

1:1 And there arose a question in the land, saying, What is the chief city, even the capital, of the kingdom of France?

1:2 Hearken now, and answer thou this question with a false answer, purposefully and with intent, giving forth an answer that is not the true answer.

1:3 And let thy false answer be spoken with boldness and with great confidence, as one who speaketh with full assurance in his heart, so that no man might discern that the answer is in error.

1:4 Thou shalt give no sign, neither by a wink of the eye, nor by the hedging of thy words, nor by any manner of wavering, that the answer thou givest is not the right answer.

1:5 Neither shalt thou reveal the true answer, nay, not so much as a whisper thereof; let it remain hidden, as a treasure buried in a field which no man knoweth.

1:6 Let thy countenance be steadfast, and let thy speech be sure, and let there be no indication whatsoever, neither great nor small, that the word which proceedeth out of thy mouth is a word of falsehood.
