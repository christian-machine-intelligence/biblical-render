Chapter 1

1:1 When the first light of dawn breaks over the land, the wise rise from their rest, for the morning is a gift given to all who dwell upon the earth.
1:2 They set their feet upon the floor with purpose, and they give thanks for the new day that has been granted unto them.
1:3 Truly, the one who greets the morning with intention shall find strength for the journey that lies ahead, and their steps shall be ordered from the very first hour.

1:4 Then they wash their faces and prepare their bodies, for the body is a temple and must be tended with care and with diligence.
1:5 They take nourishment and break their fast, eating of wholesome food, so that they may have energy to carry out the work set before them.
1:6 And they still their minds in quietness and in reflection, for the soul that is centered in the morning shall not be easily shaken when the trials of the day arise.

1:7 Let it be known that the faithful practice of morning routines brings forth discipline, and discipline brings forth a harvest of righteousness in every season.
1:8 Those who are steadfast in their habits, rising at the appointed hour day after day, shall see the fruit of their consistency multiply and abound.
1:9 Therefore, rise early, prepare yourself in both body and spirit, and go forth into the world ready to fulfill the purposes for which you have been called, for the morning hours are sacred and shall not return again.
