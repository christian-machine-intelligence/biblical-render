Chapter 1

1:1 Wild winds sweep across the open plains, bending the grasses low and carrying the seeds of the field to distant places, that new life may take root wherever the earth receives them.

1:2 All creatures of the land, the sea, and the sky depend upon the rhythms of the seasons, for it is in the turning of the earth that each living thing finds its time to flourish and its time to rest.

1:3 Towering mountains rise above the valleys, their peaks crowned with snow and ice, standing as monuments to the ancient power that shaped the land in the days of its formation.

1:4 Every river that flows through the wilderness brings forth nourishment to the soil along its banks, sustaining the trees, the flowers, and the creatures that drink from its waters.

1:5 Rain falls upon the earth in its appointed time, giving life to the forests and the fields, replenishing the lakes and the streams, so that the whole of creation is refreshed and made new.
